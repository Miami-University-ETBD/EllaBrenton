    • Some Facts About Meal Prep

    It may not seem like it but you save hundreds and evn thusands of dollars on meal prep per year.

    If we assume an average meal costs about $20, let's call that 5 meals a week. If you were to meal prep each of those instead at an average cost of $4, that would be $20 total or a $75 dollar difference.

    Meal prepping can help save money by reducing food waste, limiting impulse purchases, and streamlining grocery expenses.

    For many customers, the convenience is worth the price. On average, weekly meal prep services cost between $ 50 and $ 150 for a week's worth of meals, depending on how many people it must serve. However, some people are willing to spend $8-25 per serving for otherwise fully prepared meals, saving time and energy.

    Meal prep and meal planning both provide benefits for seniors looking to stick to a healthy diet. Meal prep provides a great way to save time and money while meal planning offers more flexibility to meet any special dietary needs. .

    Think about meal prep and how easy it'll make your life before turning away from it!

    There are very little cons to meal prep which is why we dont even pay attention to them. The pros 100% outway the cons.

    Some Pros of meal prep inlcude: Saves time: Meal prepping can save you a lot of time in the morning and at lunchtime, as you won't have to spend time deciding what to eat and cooking it. Helps with weight management: Meal prepping can help you stick to a healthy diet and lose weight, as you can plan your meals in advance and make sure you are eating healthy foods. Reduces food waste: Meal prepping can help you reduce food waste, as you will be less likely to throw away food that you don't eat. Improves diet quality: Meal prepping can help you improve your diet quality by making sure you are eating a variety of healthy foods. Reduces stress: Meal prepping can help you reduce stress by taking one less thing off your plate each day..
